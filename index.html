What is Phishing?

Phishing is a type of social engineering attack often used to steal user data, including login credentials and credit card numbers. It occurs when an attacker, masquerading as a trusted entity, tricks a victim into opening an email, instant message, or text message. The recipient is then directed to clicking a malicious link, which can lead to the installation of malware, the freezing of the system as part of a ransomware attack or the revealing of sensitive information.

How to Spot Phishing Emails

Phishing emails often appear to come from a well-known organization, such as a bank or credit card company. They usually include a link to a fake website that looks authentic and may ask for personal information, such as a password or credit card number. Here are some tips to help you spot phishing emails:

  • Check the sender's email address. Phishing emails often come from a suspicious or unfamiliar address.
  • Examine the email for generic greetings or requests for personal information. Legitimate organizations usually address you by name and never ask for sensitive information via email.
  • Look for spelling and grammar mistakes. Legitimate emails are usually well-written and free of errors.
  • Hover over links to see the actual URL. Phishing emails often contain links to fake websites.
  • Don't click on suspicious links or download attachments from unknown sources.
  • Be cautious of urgent or threatening language. Phishing emails often try to scare you into taking immediate action.

What to Do If You Suspect a Phishing Email

If you suspect that an email is a phishing attempt, do not click on any links or download any attachments. Instead, follow these steps:

  1. Do not reply to the email or forward it to others.
  2. Report the email to your organization's IT department or security team.
  3. Delete the email from your inbox and trash folder.
  4. Change your password if you clicked on any links or provided any personal information.
